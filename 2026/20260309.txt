↳ [back to full archive](2026/2026_full-archive.html)
↳ [back to index](index.html)

In Stoicism, gratitude isn't a generalized warmth toward life.
It's a moral act: discerning who gave what, what duty arises from it, and acting accordingly.
The modern "attitude of gratitude" collapses all of that into a mood.
A mood doesn't require anyone or anything specific.
!!The Stoic version does!!.
[That's the same confusion that turns Stoicism into indifference](post.html?p=2026/20260210.txt): remove the structure, keep the label.
