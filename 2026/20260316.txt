The Humean argument says: belief alone can't motivate. You also need a desire.
For factual beliefs, that's plausible. "The train leaves at six" doesn't tell you what to do.
But "this is wrong" does. The evaluative judgment already contains a direction.
!!To say something is good is already [to be pulled toward it](post.html?p=2026/20260309.txt)!!.
The desire was inside the belief, not added after.
