Utilitarianism says: the right action is the one that produces the most happiness.
Bentham took that literally. Pleasure is pleasure: a trivial game and a great book are equal, provided the amounts match.
Mill revised the theory from inside. His test: ask someone who has known both. They won't trade down voluntarily. No one who has genuinely understood something difficult will choose a simpler satisfaction in its place, not permanently.
The hierarchy isn't imposed. It [reveals itself through experience](post.html?p=2026/20260201.txt).
!!Better to be Socrates dissatisfied than a fool satisfied!!.
The fool isn't wrong about his own happiness. He just doesn't know what he's missing.
