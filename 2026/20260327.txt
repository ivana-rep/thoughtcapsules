Most moral disagreements, on inspection, are disagreements about facts. Different assumptions about consequences, history, causation. Settle the empirical question, and [the conflict often dissolves](post.html?p=2026/20260212.txt).
What remains when it doesn't: not logical error on either side. Genuine emotional differences — irreducible, not correctable by argument.
!!The first question worth asking isn't "who's right?" but "what are we actually disagreeing about?"!!
