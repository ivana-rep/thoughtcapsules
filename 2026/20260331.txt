2026_03_31 | Form, not constraint

↳ [back to index](index.html)

Aquinas doesn't treat law as an imposition on reason. He treats it as reason's own form: the shape rational order takes when it meets the world.
Where rational principle and revealed truth converge, they don't conflict. They mediate each other.
!!Law is the [architecture of right reason](post.html?p=2026/20260302.txt), not its substitute.!!
