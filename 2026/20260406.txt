'I didn't mean to' isn't a verdict — it's testimony. !!Intent is evidence in the case. It is not the verdict.!! The judgment of whether someone was harmed belongs to the person affected, not to the person [who claims good intentions](post.html?p=2026/20260405.txt). Invoking intent as exoneration doesn't just protect you from blame. It removes the other person's standing to assess what happened to them.
