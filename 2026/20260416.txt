The Stoic argument closes the space for anger. If the person who wrongs you acts rightly, there's nothing to be angry about. If they act wrongly, they do so from ignorance: a mistaken idea of what is good, which they didn't choose. In neither case does the situation require [anger](post.html?p=2026/20260121.txt). !!What looked like a target dissolves on inspection.!!
