Cruelty doesn't always require wanting harm. It can work by narrowing the overlap between yourself and another: withdrawing the recognition that keeps their suffering legible to you. Dehumanization is just that process formalized. But the direction reverses. You can widen the overlap deliberately, by choosing to look for what you share. !!How you look at someone is already an ethical act.!! Iris Murdoch called the quality of that [looking](post.html?p=2026/20260405.txt) the whole of ethics. Simone Weil called it a form of love. Both thought it precedes everything else.
