2026_06_22 | No virtue stands alone
↳ [back to full archive](2026/2026_full-archive.html)
↳ [back to index](index.html)

The same drive that got you through the hardest parts is most likely to undo you. Persistence becomes rigidity. Ambition becomes greed. Courage, without justice to temper it, becomes cruelty. The Stoics organized the virtues as a system rather than a list for a reason: none of them is stable in isolation. Each one needs the others as counterweight. !!A strength unchecked by its counterpart is the most familiar shape of failure.!!
