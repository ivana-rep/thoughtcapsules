Kant saw stupidity as a congenital deficiency: the inability to apply general concepts to specific cases. Adorno and Horkheimer gave a different account. Stupidity isn't general, they argued. It's topographic: specific regions of the mind that close off because looking there became too costly, usually early on. The question is no longer how intelligent you are. !!It's in which directions you can't look, and when that was decided for you.!!
