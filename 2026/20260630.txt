The phrase "this too shall pass" was wisdom for kings: sobering in triumph, consoling in grief. But the same quality that makes it useful in one direction makes it corrosive in the other. If everything passes, then the thing you love is already leaving. Joy becomes preemptive mourning.

The insufficiency isn't incidental. Desire is structured this way: you pursue, arrive, and find the destination was always a transit point. !!The remedy that softens suffering also erodes joy. The ring works in both directions.!!
