Pressure and excitement produce nearly identical signals in the body: elevated heart rate, sharpened focus, tension. The physiology doesn't care what you call it. What changes is your relationship to the state — which is to say, what you do next.

Some athletes, asked if they feel pressure before competition, say no. They feel energized. It's the same internal weather with a different name, and the name [shapes what happens next](post.html?p=2026/20260623.txt). !!You don't choose what rises. You choose the first word you give it.!!
