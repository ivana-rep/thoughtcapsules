Banter — the playful teasing between people who trust each other — requires conditions that can't be declared unilaterally. The same comment lands as warmth in one relationship and as wound in another. What differs isn't the words — it's the trust they fall into. "I was just joking" doesn't retroactively supply that trust.

The right to tease isn't asserted. It's established over time, by both people, through accumulated evidence of care. !!You can't claim permission you haven't been given.!!
