The demand for the optimal choice treats decision-making as a logic problem. But practical wisdom works differently: it knows full information never arrives, time is always a constraint, and "good enough" can be the result of genuine deliberation — not a failure of it.

Indecision isn't always failure. Sometimes it's deliberation still in progress. !!The benchmark isn't the best possible answer. It's the best available one, made by someone who thought it through.!!
