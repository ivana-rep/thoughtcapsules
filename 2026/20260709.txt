↳ [back to index](index.html)

Efficiency has a natural ceiling. Once you've eliminated waste, reduced friction, and streamlined the path, the gains flatten. There's a point past which there is no more efficiency to extract.

Devotion doesn't flatten. Total investment keeps generating past where optimization stopped — because it isn't drawing from the same source. Efficiency operates on the process; devotion operates on the person doing it. !!The difference between the two isn't method. It's how much of yourself you put in.!!
