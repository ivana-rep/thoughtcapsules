↳ [back to index](index.html)

The rules you live by are not truths. They're heuristics — shortcuts that were useful enough to stick, inherited from contexts you may not remember and people you may not have chosen. The problem isn't having rules. It's forgetting that's what they are.

When a rule gets mistaken for a fact, questioning it feels like instability rather than inquiry. You defend it the way you'd defend an observation. Nietzsche's eternal return works as a corrective: !!if you had to choose these rules again, infinitely, knowing what you know — would you?!! That question doesn't work on facts. Only on choices.
