Excellence, seen from the outside, looks like a sudden arrival. What doesn't get photographed are the years before it: the flat stretches, the ordinary mornings, the work done in rooms where nothing interesting was happening. Marie Curie said she never noticed what had been done; only what remained to be done. That's not modesty. It's the structure of [devotion](post.html?p=2026/20260709.txt): the distance ahead always outweighs the ground already covered.

From the inside, the exceptional looks like [boredom](post.html?p=2026/20260615.txt). That's also why so few people stay.

!!Success is built in rooms no one would photograph.!!
