Naval Ravikant, [entrepreneur and investor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Ravikant) known for his writing on wealth and self-knowledge, makes a distinction worth sitting with. Specific knowledge is what you do better than anyone else in a way that's genuinely yours and can't be taught directly. The mistake is thinking you can locate it through introspection.

You can't. It shows up in action: in difficult situations where you notice you're navigating something others can't, or when someone says *I don't know how you do that.* His example is an entrepreneur who can make 400 cold calls, absorb 399 rejections without flinching, and consider a single yes enough to start. That resistance to rejection isn't a skill you develop. It's something you [discover only by doing](post.html?p=2026/20260409.txt).

!!You don't find your specific knowledge by thinking about it. You stumble on it.!!
