Every app wants a decision in seconds. Every employer wants quarterly results. Every news feed earns more when you act before you've thought. Impatience isn't a character flaw that some people happen to have. It's [the normal output of normal systems](post.html?p=2026/20260312.txt), designed to extract a specific behavior.

The cost is invisible because you pay it in what you don't get. The investment sold too early, the decision made on impulse, the conversation you needed to have but didn't: these don't register as impatience. They register as bad luck.

!!The patient inherit everything the impatient leave behind.!!
