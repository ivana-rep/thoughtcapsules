Most people allocate the wrong way: endless punishment for the past, unlimited leniency for the present. The guilt doesn't produce change; the excuse doesn't require any.

A more useful distribution: be lenient with your past self, take the lessons and leave the blame. Be strict with your present self, because this is the only moment you can actually influence. Be flexible with your future self: many paths lead to the same place, and you're not obligated to have predicted which one.

[Nostalgia](post.html?p=2026/20260401.txt) runs the same mistake in reverse, reconstructing the past charitably and using it as evidence. Either way, you're treating your past self as villain or saint when they were just you, earlier.

!!The single constant across all three tenses is you. That's where the problem is, and the only place the solution can be.!!
