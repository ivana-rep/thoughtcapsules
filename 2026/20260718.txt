The comparison trap has a structural flaw: you see someone else's output and compare it to your own input. Their result against your process. You don't know what it cost them, how long it took, or what they've sacrificed in the parts you can't see. The conclusions are always wrong.

Beyond that: every second spent comparing is a second not building. Charlie Munger, [investor and thinker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlie_Munger) known for his mental models, made [opportunity cost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opportunity_cost) a default mode of thinking: every choice is only understood relative to what you're not doing instead. Comparison is an expensive choice disguised as free information.

!!Your lane is the only one where your effort [compounds](post.html?p=2026/20260507.txt).!!
