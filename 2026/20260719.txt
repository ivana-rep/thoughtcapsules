The fundamentals are known to almost everyone. That's what makes them rare in practice. Most people understand that consistency matters, that reliability is underrated, that being genuinely pleasant to work with has lasting effects. Very few do it consistently, because none of it is interesting to perform.

Three qualities that make someone hard to replace, with no connection to talent or intelligence: being a net positive on the energy of any room they're in, taking full ownership of their part without needing to be reminded, and finding a path forward when conditions aren't ideal rather than waiting for them to improve.

None of these require exceptional ability. !!They require a steady willingness that turns out to be surprisingly uncommon.!!
