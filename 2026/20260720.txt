What makes a cognitive trap a trap is that it doesn't announce itself. The perfectionist doesn't experience perfectionism; they experience being thorough. The procrastinator isn't avoiding; they're preparing. "Someday" doesn't feel like postponement. It feels like strategy: wait for the right conditions, the right moment, the right version of yourself.

The conditions never arrive, because the habit of waiting is also the habit of not recognizing when conditions are adequate. The trap is self-sealing.

!!Most difficulty doesn't come from the situation itself, but from [a story about it](post.html?p=2026/20260131.txt) that feels like clarity.!!
