Experience doesn't teach automatically. The feedback is always there. Patterns recur, outcomes repeat. But the conclusions people draw are often not the lesson; they're the confirmation of what was already believed. The information arrives and gets routed through the existing framework.

The person who makes the same mistake for ten years isn't missing data. They're missing the disposition to let it land. [How you hold a belief](post.html?p=2026/20260707.txt) determines what you can learn from experience, not the experience itself.

!!You can accumulate decades of evidence and emerge more entrenched. The deciding factor isn't what happened to you.!!
