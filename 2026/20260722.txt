When private equity enters a football club, the metrics improve first: revenue, stadium utilization, brand reach. What doesn't appear in the data is what's quietly eroding: the sense of belonging, the intergenerational ritual, the identity investment that made the club worth acquiring.

The fan is not a customer. A customer is satisfied by a transaction. The fan has attached part of their [identity](post.html?p=2026/20260208.txt) to the outcome of a game, to a jersey color, a city, a collective memory. That attachment isn't extractable. It's the source of everything that can be extracted.

!!The structural paradox: optimizing for return is the fastest way to destroy what you paid for.!!
