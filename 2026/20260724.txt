What automation replaces first is the structured, procedural content of any profession: the part that can be reduced to protocol, to rule-following, to sequence. The protocol survives; the person following it becomes redundant.

What isn't replaceable is what was never reducible to protocol in the first place: the ability to read a room, to translate between people who aren't saying quite what they mean, to hold a relationship across friction and time. Architects know how to explain buildings. Lawyers know how to argue without proof. These are forms, ways of being in a situation, not just content. The content is already in the machine.

!!What you do is increasingly beside the point. How you are while you do it is what remains.!!
