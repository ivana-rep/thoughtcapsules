Aquinas builds from a metaphysics of the good. The Stoics build from the distinction between what's up to you and what isn't. Opposite starting points, incompatible premises, different languages entirely.

Their practical psychology of [anger](post.html?p=2026/20260209.txt) arrives at the same place: the emotion becomes destructive when it acts before reason has assessed the situation. You misidentify the target, misjudge the proportion, move before you understand what you're reacting to.

!!When incompatible systems arrive at the same practical prescription, that prescription carries more weight than either system alone.!!
