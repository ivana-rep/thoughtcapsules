↳ [back to index](index.html)

Every ritual exists somewhere between symbol and act. A marriage ceremony isn't just a public announcement of a decision already made. It constitutes a new legal and social fact. An oath isn't a description of intention but an intention made binding by the saying of it.

The Eucharist is the most extreme version of this question: is the bread a representation of Christ's body, or the body itself? The theological tradition that insists on real presence understands what's at stake. A symbol commemorates. A sacrament changes the reality it addresses.

!!Some acts don't describe what is. They determine what becomes.!!
